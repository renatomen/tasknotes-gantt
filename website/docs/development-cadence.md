# Development Cadence

*An honest note about time, love, and realistic expectations.* 💜

Hi. Before you get too invested, here's a straight-up note about how this plugin
is looked after — so nothing about the pace catches you by surprise.

I built TaskNotes Gantt to **scratch my own itch**. I wanted to see my TaskNotes
on a proper timeline, so I made one: opinionated, shaped around the way *I* work,
and tended like a small garden I get to on weekends. I love it dearly. I also
have very little time for it.

So here's the honest deal:

- **What's here is solid.** Every shipped feature is backed by thorough unit and
  integration tests, plus a lot of my own real-world use. Nothing here is
  half-baked — if it's in the plugin, it works.
- **The *support*, though, is beta.** That's the real reason the plugin still
  wears a Beta label. Not because the code is shaky — because *I* can't promise
  to be fast. Bug reports, feature requests, and suggestions may sit for a while
  before I get to them. I will genuinely do my best to reply as quickly as I can
  — but "as I can" may be slower than you'd like, and I'd much rather tell you
  that up front than leave you refreshing an issue thread.
- **I'm keeping a fairly tight hand on the wheel — for now.** I'm not expanding
  the scope much, and I'm holding firm, opinionated control over where it goes.
  Not out of ego; purely out of bandwidth.

None of that is where I want things to *stay*. My hope is for this to become far
more **community-driven** over time — to co-evolve alongside the wonderful
**[TaskNotes](https://tasknotes.dev)** plugin, and maybe to grow the standalone
(TaskNotes-free) side into something richer. I'd be genuinely open to
collaborators down the road; I'm simply too stretched to coordinate that well
today.

If you found your way here because something's broken or missing: thank you,
truly, for your patience. This little plugin is made with a great deal of care
and not a great deal of spare time — and I'm really glad it's useful to you. 🙏
